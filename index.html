Introduction

I am Surhud Naik and i just completed my first year in Btech. Mechanical Engineering at IIT Bombay. It was not more than a year ago that I joined this institute, but it feels like I have been here for a long time. The first year at IIT Bombay was a enriching to say the least and was certainly a year to remember. I made a lot of new friends, learned a lot of new things, and had a lot of fun. I am from Mumbai itself so there was less of a culture shock for me, as it was for many of my to-be friends who had come from lesser known villages and towns across the country. The first year was a time of exploration, where I got to know the campus, the people, and the culture of IIT Bombay.

Activities

I was an active participant in various activities and events, most notable of which are those which were conducted by Symphony, the Music Club of IIT Bombay. I participated in the annual IITB band competition, Battle of the Bands, where I formed a band with other musically gifted students of my batch and played a medley of 3 songs. I also performed as a drummer in Surbahaar, the annual Musical Showcase where I was a part of the Garaj Garaj Rock band along with other senior members of Symphony.
DevCom was one of the few clubs that caught my attention since the start, and I went on to develop an interest in web development. I learned HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, and created a few projects in the course of the recruitment process for DevCom. I was finally selected after 3 months of the rigorous recruitment process, and I am now a member of the DevCom team as a developer.
I also volunteered as an organiser for TechFest, the annual technical festival of IIT Bombay, where I helped in organising Robowars and controlling the console during the main event for the three days of the festival. Since all my friends from mumbai since the JEE days also had holidays and were home, we were able to ennjoy Mood Indigo together.
I got the rare chance of being a part of Goonj, the annual music hostel GC, where I represented Hostel 17 as a drummer and composed a song together with them. It was a great experience to work with Mtech students of the campus and get exposed to their wide wisdom and experience.

Learnings

The first year at IIT Bombay was a time of learning and growth. I learned a lot about myself, my interests, and my passions. I learned how to manage my time effectively, how to work in a team, and how to balance academics with extracurricular activities.
IIT Bombay exposed me to a lot of new ideas and perspectives, and I learned to think critically and creatively. I also learned the importance of networking and building relationships with people from different backgrounds and cultures. I learnt to live independently, managing my own finances, and taking care of my own laundry.
I was allotted H1, which as everyone on campus knows, is an adjustement to live in, but I saw it turn into a new home away from home. In the end, the core lesson I learnt was that of getting by in life when you are your own support system owning up to yourself and your actions, and being responsible for your own decisions.
